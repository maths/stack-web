# Online Assessment in University STEM Education in Scotland with STACK

This was a day of practical workshops and planning meetings to develop plans and concrete activities to generate impact from the STACK online assessment system primarily in Scotland.

**Date:**  30th April 2026.<br/>
**Venue:** Earl Mountbatten Building, School of Mathematical and Computer Sciences, Riccarton Campus, [Heriot-Watt University](https://www.hw.ac.uk/campuses/edinburgh/maps-directions). 

This open session was intended to: 

* Share recent developments in STACK, open materials, and tools for collaboration  
* Explore how online assessment can support STEM education in Scottish universities 
* Provide a practical introduction to authoring STACK questions (for new or prospective users) 
* Build a collaborative network across institutions interested in scalable, research-informed assessment 

**Timetable:**

13:45-15:00   Hands-on Authoring Workshop<br/>
15:00-15:30   Coffee<br/>

The event was attended by colleagues in university mathematics, physics, engineering and related disciplines who are interested in: 

* Online or blended assessment 
* Scaling high-quality mathematical feedback 
* Open educational resource development 
* Cross-institutional collaboration in STEM education

# Executive Summary: STACK Workshop

The STACK workshop brought together colleagues from across Scotland to share their experiences and explore how we can work more closely to support STEM students through digital assessment. Our conversations moved beyond technical "how-to" sessions toward a shared vision of assessment as a progression tool that supports learning at every stage—from foundational bridging modules to advanced honours courses. By pooling our collective expertise and resources, particularly within the [Maxwell Institute](https://www.maxwell.ac.uk/), we aim to build a sustainable network that reduces individual workload while addressing shared challenges like academic integrity, resource equity, and the transition from legacy systems.

### Theme 1: Operations and Scaling

* Invigilation Models: Discussion centered on whether the strict "same time, same place" invigilation required for paper exams is necessary for online delivery.
* Capacity Challenges: Physical room sizes and resourcing limitations frequently necessitate multiple exam sittings, complicating the logistics of high-stakes assessment.
* International Equity: Significant equipment disparities exist between campuses in Malaysia, Dubai, and Edinburgh, raising concerns about geographical fairness.
* Integrity and Browsers: There is a clear shift from open-book home exams back to closed-browser environments to ensure learning outcomes are actually being met.

### Theme 2: Pedagogy and Coursework Design

* Mastery Learning: Participants proposed a model where students must achieve 80% on a "Practice Quiz" with unlimited attempts before they can access a final, single-attempt test.
* Feedback Loops: Effective assessment should provide detailed, standardized feedback that identifies similar mistakes to encourage repeated attempts and mastery.
* Advanced Applications: While established in early-year math, colleagues are eager to adapt STACK for advanced courses involving proof-based content and problem-solving in applied settings.
* Balanced Engagement: It is vital to manage the volume of STACK feedback to avoid overwhelming students within a single course.

### Theme 3: The "Foundation Bridge" and Student Support

* Motivation Concerns: Discussions highlighted the need for diagnostic testing and timetabling to encourage engagement, as purely self-paced courses often see low participation.
* Individualized Learning: Diagnostic scores could be used to tailor course content to a student's specific knowledge gaps, particularly when entry-level knowledge varies significantly.
* Gamification and Tutoring: Gamified elements and proactive tutor follow-ups were suggested as methods to maintain student momentum.

### Theme 4: Sharing Materials and STEM Equity

* Repository Design: There is strong interest in a central repository containing standard questions, direct libraries, and clear guidance on copyright and licensing.
* Quality Control: The group suggested using usage data to build quality control mechanisms to identify the most effective shared content.
* National Outreach: STACK is currently supporting a variety of projects, including the Scholar programme, Strathclyde’s alternative pathway summer schools, and CPD for Advanced Higher qualifications.
* Access Barriers: A persistent challenge is ensuring students across Scotland have equal access to the platforms and equipment required for these resources.
